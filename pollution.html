Environmental Pollution

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or energy into the environment, leading to adverse effects on air, water, land, plants, animals, and human health. It is one of the most pressing global issues of the 21st century.

What is Pollution?

Pollution refers to contamination of natural resources such as air, water, and soil. These contaminants may include chemicals, waste, particulate matter, or noise, which degrade the quality of the environment and disrupt the balance of ecosystems.

Types of Pollution

  • Air Pollution: Pollutants released into the air affect respiratory health and contribute to global warming.
  • Water Pollution: Contaminated water bodies harm aquatic life and reduce safe drinking water availability.
  • Soil Pollution: Toxic substances in soil affect farming and enter the food chain.
  • Noise Pollution: Excess sound levels impact mental and physical wellbeing.

Causes of Pollution

Effects on Human Health

Exposure to pollution can lead to respiratory disorders, cardiovascular diseases, weakened immune systems, and neurological problems. Long-term exposure may result in chronic illness or reduced life expectancy. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Solutions and Preventive Measures

  • Promote clean energy (solar, wind power)
  • Enforce pollution control regulations
  • Reduce plastic usage and improve waste management
  • Plant trees and improve green cover
  • Encourage public transport and electric vehicles
