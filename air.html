Air Pollution

Definition

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances such as gases, dust, smoke, and particulate matter in the atmosphere that are injurious to human health and the environment.

Major Causes of Air Pollution

  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial activities
  • Burning of fossil fuels
  • Construction and mining
  • Agricultural practices

Effects of Air Pollution

  • Respiratory diseases such as asthma
  • Climate change and global warming
  • Damage to plants and ecosystems
  • Reduced visibility and air quality

Prevention and Control Measures

  • Use of public transport and EVs
  • Adoption of renewable energy
  • Plantation and green belts
  • Strict pollution control laws
