Air Pollution

Definition

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances such as gases, dust, smoke, and chemicals in the atmosphere that are injurious to human health and the environment.

Major Causes of Air Pollution

  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial activities
  • Burning of fossil fuels
  • Construction and mining activities
  • Agricultural practices

Effects of Air Pollution

  • Respiratory diseases such as asthma and bronchitis
  • Global warming and climate change
  • Damage to plants, animals, and ecosystems
  • Reduction in air quality and visibility

Prevention and Control Measures

  • Use of public transport and electric vehicles
  • Adoption of renewable energy sources
  • Planting more trees
  • Strict environmental laws and policies
